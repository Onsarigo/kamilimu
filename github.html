Why Build the Open-Source Way?

Control: Gain more control over the software and how it functions.

Security: Easily identify and fix errors and omissions.

Stability:Ensure tools remain available even if the original creators move on.

Training: Enhance learning through hands-on experience and study.

Community: Foster a vibrant community of users and developers around your projects.

Need For Open-Source

The aim of open source is to foster collaboration and innovation by making software and its source code freely available to the public. This approach encourages a diverse community of developers to contribute, improve, and customize the software, enhancing transparency, security, and trust. Open source accelerates the development of new features and technologies, makes high-quality software accessible to everyone, and provides valuable learning opportunities. Ultimately, it builds a supportive network of users and developers who drive projects forward collectively, ensuring long-term stability and growth.

OPEN-SOURCE ECOSYSTEM

  • Author: The person/s or organization that created the project.
  • Owner: The person/s who has administrative ownership over the organization or repository (not always the same as the original author)
  • Maintainers: Contributors who are responsible for driving the vision and managing the organizational aspects of the project (They may also be authors or owners of the project.)
  • Contributors: Everyone who has contributed something back to the project.
